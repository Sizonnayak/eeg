┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 3: BANDPASS FILTERING                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Filter specifications:
  Type:       FIR (Finite Impulse Response)
  Low cutoff: 0.1 Hz
  High cutoff: 30 Hz
  Window:     Hamming
  Phase:      Zero-phase (linear phase delay removed)
  Order:      Auto-computed for 1-second kernel

Frequency band rationale:
  - 0.1 Hz highpass: Preserves slow DC shifts (P300 has baseline deviation)
  - 30 Hz lowpass: Removes EMG, line noise (50/60 Hz), high-freq artifacts
  - P300 peak: ~2-5 Hz (captured well in 0.1-30 Hz band)

Changes from v1:
  - v1: Subject-specific (0.5-20 Hz or 1-30 Hz)
  - v2: UNIFORM 0.1-30 Hz across all subjects
  - Rationale: Lower highpass (0.1 vs 0.5/1.0) preserves slow P300 components

Code:
  raw.filter(l_freq=0.1, h_freq=30.0, method='fir', fir_window='hamming')


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 4: COMMON AVERAGE REFERENCE (CAR)                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Method: Re-reference to average of all 8 channels

Formula:
  X_CAR(t, ch) = X(t, ch) - mean(X(t, :))

  Where:
    X(t, ch) = voltage at time t, channel ch
    mean(X(t, :)) = average voltage across all 8 channels at time t

Benefits:
  1. Reduces common-mode noise (affects all electrodes equally)
  2. Reduces electrode drift (slow DC shifts shared across channels)
  3. Enhances spatial resolution (highlights local activity)
  4. Standard practice for P300 BCIs

Effect on amplitude:
  - Mean across channels becomes 0 at each time point
  - Typical range post-CAR: ±50 µV (reduced from ±100 µV raw)

Code:
  raw.set_eeg_reference(ref_channels='average', projection=False)


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 5: EPOCHING & BASELINE CORRECTION                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Epoch extraction:
  Pre-stimulus:  200 ms (50 samples @ 250 Hz)
  Post-stimulus: 800 ms (200 samples @ 250 Hz)
  Total window:  1000 ms (250 samples)
  Time-locked to: Stimulus onset (flash event)

Epoch structure:
  t = -200ms to +800ms
  Sample 0-49:   Pre-stimulus baseline
  Sample 50:     Stimulus onset (t=0)
  Sample 51-249: Post-stimulus response

Baseline correction:
  Baseline window: -200ms to 0ms (samples 0-49)
  Method: Subtract pre-stimulus mean per channel

  Formula:
    X_corrected(ch, t) = X(ch, t) - mean(X(ch, 0:49))

  Rationale: Removes DC offsets and slow drifts present before stimulus

Expected P300 timing:
  - Onset: ~200-250ms (samples 100-112)
  - Peak: ~300-500ms (samples 125-175)
  - Duration: ~200-300ms
  - Amplitude: 5-15 µV positive deflection (post-baseline correction)

Code:
  epoch = X_filtered[onset - PRE_SAMPLES : onset + POST_SAMPLES, :]
  baseline = epoch[:PRE_SAMPLES, :].mean(axis=0)
  epoch -= baseline


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 6: ARTIFACT REJECTION                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Method: Amplitude threshold (simple, effective)

Criterion:
  Reject epoch if: max(|amplitude|) > threshold

Threshold per subject (optimized during EDA):
  S01: 150 µV  (standard, clean signal)
  S02: 200 µV  (higher, very clean signal)
  S03: 100 µV  (lower, more artifacts)
  S04: 100 µV  (lower, more artifacts)
  S05: 100 µV  (lower, more artifacts)
  S06: 150 µV  (standard)
  S07: 150 µV  (standard)
  S08: 150 µV  (standard)

Rationale for subject-specific thresholds:
  - Adaptive to individual signal quality
  - S03/S04/S05 had more artifacts (stricter rejection)
  - S02 very clean (lenient threshold)

Rejection rate: ~5-10% of total epochs

Code:
  if np.abs(epoch).max() > artifact_threshold:
      n_rejected += 1
      continue  # Skip this epoch

================================================================================
  SIGNAL PROCESSING RATIONALE
================================================================================

WHY 0.1-30 Hz?
--------------
P300 frequency content:
  - Dominant: 2-5 Hz (slow positive wave)
  - Baseline deviation: 0.1-1 Hz (DC shift)
  - Harmonics: up to 10 Hz

Noise sources to remove:
  - High-frequency EMG: >30 Hz
  - Line noise: 50/60 Hz
  - Very low drift: <0.1 Hz (non-neural)

WHY COMMON AVERAGE REFERENCE?
------------------------------
Traditional reference:
  - Mastoid/ear: Not available in this dataset
  - Linked mastoids: Gold standard but not always practical

CAR advantages for P300:
  - Reference-free (no need for extra electrodes)
  - Reduces common-mode noise (affects all channels)
  - Standard in BCI literature
  - Works well with limited channels (8)

WHY BASELINE CORRECTION?
-------------------------
Pre-stimulus baseline removal:
  - Eliminates DC offsets before each trial
  - Removes slow drifts that vary trial-to-trial
  - Makes P300 amplitude comparable across epochs
  - Standard practice in ERP analysis

WHY ARTIFACT REJECTION?
------------------------
Amplitude threshold is simple but effective:
  - Eye blinks: >100 µV (frontal channels)
  - Muscle artifacts: >150 µV
  - Motion artifacts: Variable, >100 µV

Subject-specific thresholds:
  - Adapts to individual signal quality
  - Keeps more good epochs while rejecting true artifacts
  - Optimized via visual inspection in EDA
